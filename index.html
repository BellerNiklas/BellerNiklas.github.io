About

I am a Master's student in Economics at the University of Bonn, currently on an exchange semester at ENSAE Paris. My academic focus is on quantitative and empirical methods in economics, with particular interests in macroeconomics, labor economics, and public policy.

I have extensive research experience working with leading economists on topics ranging from inflation dynamics and asset returns to capital gains taxation and household finance. My work combines rigorous economic theory with advanced econometric methods and large-scale data analysis. I am proficient in R, MATLAB, Stata, and Python.

Prior to my Master's studies, I completed my Bachelor's degree in Economics at the University of Mannheim. I am a merit-based scholarship recipient of the Friedrich-Ebert-Foundation.

Research Interests

  • Labor Economics and Public Policy
  • Applied Econometrics and Quantitative Methods
